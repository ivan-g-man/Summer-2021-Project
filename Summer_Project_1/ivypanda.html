ü§î How to Summarize an Article without Plagiarizing?

Of course, there are times when you can‚Äôt depend on online tools. For example, you may be restricted to use them in a class or maybe you have to highlight some specific paragraphs and customizing the tool‚Äôs settings would take more time and efforts than summary writing itself.

In this chapter, you‚Äôll learn to summarize a long article, essay, research paper, report, or a book chapter with the help of helpful tips, a logical approach, and a little bit of creativity.

Here are some methods to let you create a fantastic summary.

  1. Know your goal. To choose the right route to your goal, you need to understand it perfectly. Why should you summarize the text? What is its style: scientific or publicistic? Who is the author? Where was the article published? There are many significant questions that can help to adapt your text better. Develop a short interview to use during the summary writing. Include all the important information on where you need to post the text and for what purpose.
  2. Thorough reading. To systemize your thoughts about the text, it‚Äôs significant to investigate it in detail. Read the text two or more times to grasp the basic ideas of the article and understand its goals and motives.
    Give yourself all the time you need to process the text. Often we need a couple of hours to extract the right results from the study or learn to paraphrase the text properly.
  3. Highlight the main idea. When writing a summary, you bear a responsibility for the author. Not only you have to extract the significant idea of the text but to paraphrase it correctly. It‚Äôs important not to misrepresent any of the author‚Äôs conclusions in your summary. That‚Äôs why you should find the main idea and make sure, you can paraphrase it without a loss of meaning.
    If possible, read a couple of professional reviews of a targeted book chapter or article. It can help you to analyze the text better.
  4. Mark the arguments. The process of summarizing is always easier if you have a marker to highlight important details in the text. If you don‚Äôt have a printed text, there‚Äôs always Microsoft Word to use a highlight tool on the paper.
    Try to mark all arguments, statistics, and facts in the text to represent them in your summary. This information will turn into key elements of the summary you‚Äôll create, so keep attention on what you highlight exactly.
  5. Take care of plagiarism. Before you start writing, learn what percent of originality should you aim at. Various projects have different requirements. And they determine how many efforts you should put into writing to get a perfect summary your teacher will like.
    Depending on the percent of originality, build a plan for your short text. Allow yourself copy as much information as allowed to save your time.
  6. Build a structure. With the help of key elements, which you‚Äôve highlighted in the text, it‚Äôs possible to create a powerful structure including all the interesting facts and arguments.
    Develop an outline according to a basic structure ‚Äì introduction, body, and conclusion. Even if your summary is extremely short, the main idea should sound in both the first and last sentences.
  7. Write a draft. If you‚Äôre not a professional writer, it can be extremely difficult to develop a text with the correct word count on the first try. We advise you to develop a general text firstly ‚Äì include all the information without controlling the number of sentences.
  8. Cut out the unnecessary parts. On this step, you should edit the draft and eliminate the unnecessary parts. Keep in mind, the number of sentences your summary must contain. Make sure the main point is fully represented in the text. You can cut out any sentence except those concluding the significant arguments.
  9. Proofread your summary. We recommend making a thorough proofread of your summary. Here are the main aspects to check in your text:
    • Wordiness ‚Äì you should delete unnecessary words, which make it difficult to understand the text
    • Common mistakes ‚Äì mistakes made in academic papers are basically the same, so it‚Äôs helpful to have an article like this one when you‚Äôre proofreading
    • Appropriate terminology ‚Äì for each topic, there‚Äôs a list of the terminology you can use
    • Facts and statistics ‚Äì you can accidently write a wrong year or percent, make sure to avoid these mistakes
    • Quotes ‚Äì every quote should be written correctly and have a link to its source.

üìù How to Proofread Your Summary?

Now, when you know how to summarize an article, it‚Äôs time to edit your text whether it‚Äôs your own writing or a summary generator‚Äôs results.

In this chapter, you‚Äôll see the basic ways to proofread any type of text: academic paper (essay, research paper, etc.), article, letter, book‚Äôs chapter, and so on.

  1. Proofread your summary. Are there times when you can‚Äôt remember an appropriate synonym? Then you should use Thesaurus and analogous services from time to time.
    They can expand your vocabulary a lot and help to find the right words even in the most challenging situations.
  2. Pay attention to easily confused words. It‚Äôs especially significant if you edit a nonfiction text ‚Äì there‚Äôs a number of words people often confuse without even realizing. English Oxford Living Dictionaries have a list of these word pairs so you won‚Äôt miss any.
  3. Proofread one type of mistakes at a time. To edit a paper properly don‚Äôt split your attention to grammar and punctuation‚Äîthis way you can miss dozens of mistakes. To get more accurate results, read the first time to edit the style, the second to eliminate grammar mistakes, and the third to proofread punctuation.   4. Take a rest from your paper. If you use an online summarizing tool, you can skip this step. But if you‚Äôve been writing a paper for several hours and now trying to edit it without taking a break, it may be a bad idea. Why? Because without a fresh pair of eyes there‚Äôs a great possibility not to spot even obvious mistakes.
    Give yourself some time to slightly forget the text‚Äîgo for a walk or call a friend, and then return to work as a new person.
  5. Hire a proofreader. If you need to get perfect results, think about hiring a professional. Skills and qualification, which they have, guarantee a perfect text without any mistakes or style issues. Once you find a proofreader, you can optimize your work perfectly.
    Search for specialists on freelance websites like UpWork ‚Äî it‚Äôs comfortable and safe to use. Of course, there‚Äôs one flaw you should think about‚Äîhiring a pro is expensive. So, everyone should decide on their own whether they need to spend this money or not.
  6. Switch your paper with a friend. If you can‚Äôt afford a professional editor, there‚Äôs a less expensive option‚Äîask a friend to look through your paper and proofread theirs in return.
    Make sure, you both make manual editing, not just check it with Microsoft Office or analogous software. Although there are great grammar tools, they still can‚Äôt spot many mistakes obvious to a human.
  7. Use grammar checking tools. We recommend you not to depend on multiple grammar tools. But the assistance it can offer is irreplaceable. Start your proofreading by scanning your text with Grammarly or an analogous tool.
    The service detects many types of errors including confusing words‚Äô pairs, punctuation, misspellings, wordiness, incorrect word order, unfinished sentences, and so on.

  8. Read aloud. It‚Äôs amazing how different the written text can sound when read aloud. If you practice this proofreading method, you know that many mistakes can be spotted if you actually pronounce the text.
    Why does it happen? People understand information better if they perceive it with the help of different senses. You can use this trick even in learning‚Äî memorize the materials with the help of reading, listening, and speaking.

Text Summarizing FAQs

üìå How to write an academic summary?
An academic summary is a broad overview of the source. To make an article summary, you need to put its main ideas into your own words. A summary shouldn't be extensive. Still, it takes time to write a perfect one. The key to success is reading the source thoroughly. You need to make sure that you understand the main ideas of the text very well.
üìå How to summarize without plagiarizing?
  • Use your own words. Try to avoid paraphrasing the text sentence by sentence.
  • Use special software to check your summary for plagiarism. There are many free plagiarism checkers to be found online.
  • Do not forget that presenting other authors‚Äô ideas without any reference to them, even when done in your own words, is also plagiarism. Be sure to refer to the source when working on your text.
üìå How to summarize in your own words?
  • Read the text thoroughly to make sure that you understand its main idea quite well.
  • Write an introduction. Formulate there the main idea of the text. Articulate it very clearly, make a short statement.
  • Put your source away and write the body of your summary without looking into it. If you feel that you‚Äôve forgotten something important, go back to the source and reread the whole story. Then go on working on your summary.
üìå Which linguistic tools help in summarizing a text?
Automated tools that help in summarizing a text are called summary generators. There are plenty of free online text summarizers to be found on the web. The general principle of these tools is quite simple. You paste the text you need to summarize into a box, set the desired number of words, get your summary, and enjoy the results!
üìå What is the purpose of summarizing an essay?
The purpose of an essay summary is to present the most important points and arguments of a text. A good summary is short and logical. Imagine that you need to describe the author‚Äôs intent to someone who has not read the essay. To make a good summary, you need to read the work thoroughly.
